Sandy Ong

Science Journalist

Sandy is a science journalist based in New York City. She holds a BSc in life sciences, received a master’s degree in forensic science and is currently working on a MA in journalism at New York University. Once upon a time, she was a competitive tennis player, mindless lab rat and unfulfilled medical writer. Now, Sandy tells stories about science — so much of it fascinates her that she’s written about a range of things from the psychology of seeking closure to having an eco-friendly burial. Away from writing, Sandy works on her bucket list, which mostly involves travelling to unexplored places in new countries — 31 and counting. She’s lived on three continents and most recently moved across the pond from London. Culturally confused/complex, she says “lift,” “loo” and “lah.” Born on a tropical island one degree north of the equator, sunshine makes her very happy.
